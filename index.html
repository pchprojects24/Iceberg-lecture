Iceberg Fundamentals

  • Iceberg: Glacier-born freshwater ice with at least 5 m above sea level.
  • Sea ice: Frozen seawater (not glacier-derived).
  • Calving: The break-off process that creates icebergs.
  • Hidden mass: 50–90% of an iceberg sits underwater.
  • Color clue: Deep blue = dense ice with fewer air bubbles.
  • Source glaciers: Petermann, Humboldt, Yancheng, and others in Greenland.
  • Calving highlights: Large Petermann events in 2010 and 2012.
  • Fun fact: Icebergs near oil platforms are sometimes towed away!

Drift & Origins

Birth: Calved from Greenland glaciers into Baffin Bay.
Drift: Ride the Baffin Bay and Labrador currents for 2–3 years.
Arrival: Reach Canadian waters & “Iceberg Alley.”
Sea ice shield: Heavier sea ice can protect icebergs.

Western Arctic update: 2019–2023 warming triggered ice island fragments that drifted into new shipping lanes, forcing updates (and sometimes removals) of traditional iceberg limit lines.

Observation & Reporting

  • Canadian Ice Service + US International Ice Patrol coordinate tracking.
  • Satellite imagery, direct observation, and public reports all help.
  • Reporting needs only location + size (anyone can report).
  • Iceberg monitoring intensified after the Titanic disaster.
  • Large, flat icebergs are preferred for scientific equipment placement.
  • Helicopter landings work best on medium-to-large flat icebergs.

Workshop Takeaways

  • Western Arctic iceberg limits are evolving alongside climate change.
  • Anyone can report sightings; all observations are valuable.
  • Ice islands and large flat bergs are prime targets for science deployments.
